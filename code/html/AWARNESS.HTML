- Protect your privacy online:

1.Passwords:


• Creating strong and secure passwords is a must for protecting your personal information online.
Weak passwords, such as "Password1" or "12345," can be easily guessed by hackers in less than a second.
To ensure the security of your online accounts,
it is important to create passwords that are difficult for others to guess.

2. Protecting Your Privacy on Social Media


- When it comes to protecting your privacy on social media platforms like Facebook,
there are several steps you can take to ensure your personal information remains secure
Here are some tips to keep in mind:

• Set your profile to private: Adjust your privacy settings on
Facebook to ensure that only people you know in real life can see your profile
and posts. This will help prevent, strangers from accessing your personal information.

• Be mindful of what you post: Avoid sharing sensitive personal information
such as your date of birth, address, or pictures of your vacation while you are away.
By being cautious about the content you share,
you can minimize the risk of your information falling into the wrong hands.
Review your privacy settings: Take the time to review and customize your
privacy settings on social media platforms. This includes
choosing who can see your posts and profile,
as well as limiting who can send you connection requests.

Multifactor Authentication:

• For this reason, practically every online service—including banking, social media, shopping,
and, yes, Microsoft 365—has enhanced account security. The term "multifactor authentication" or
"two-step verification" may be used, but the best ones follow the same basic idea. More information is
required when logging into your account for the first time on a new device or app (such as a web browser).

• To establish your identity, a second means of verification, or what we refer to as a second "factor" is required.
When you attempt to log in, a factor in authentication serves as a means of verifying your identity.
One type of factor is something you know, like a password.
The three most common kinds of factors are:

1-Something you know - Like a password, or a memorized PIN.
2-Something you have - Like a smartphone, or a secure USB key.
3-Something you are - Like a fingerprint, or facial recognition.

Keep your child safe online:

1.Set clear ground rules

• Have sincere conversations with your kids about who and how they communicate,
as well as who can view what they publish online.
• Tell your child as soon as possible to you or another trusted adult
if they come across anything on the internet that worries, upsets, or frightens them.

2. Use technology to protect them

• Verify that the device your child is using is up to date, that its security settings are turned on,
and that it is set up to minimize data collection
so that people cannot view any information that you simply do not need them to see.
• Teach your kids the value of protecting personal information.
Your data is visible to everybody if your security settings are not safe.

3. Spend time with them online

• Provide opportunities for your child to interact safely and positively with friends,
family, and you online. Engaging in social interactions can present an
excellent chance for you to express empathy and compassion in a virtual environment.
